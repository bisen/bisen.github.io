PICOTS Inclusion Exclusion
Population Non-pregnant community-dwelling adult women with symptoms of UI  If >10% of study participants are from ineligible groups (children or adolescents, men, pregnant women, institutionalized or hospitalized participants, or have surgically-treated UI) 
Subpopulations:
•women engaging in athletic activity, 
•older women 
•women in the military or veterans
•racial and ethnic minorities
Interventions Nonpharmacological interventions: Behavioral interventions, neuromodulation, intravesical pressure release devices, and combinations therereof  Interventions not available in the United States and surgical treatments
Pharmacological interventions: Anticholinergics, onabotulinum toxin A (BTX), hormones; alpha agonists, beta agonists; antidepressants, periurethral bulking agents (see full report)
Combinations of eligible nonpharmacological and pharmacological interventions.
All doses or variations of interventions are included, including unapproved doses. Similarly, all eligible interventions are included regardless of regulatory body approval.
Comparators Any eligible intervention, sham or no treatment.  
Outcomes Categorical measures of UI: Cure*, improvement, satisfaction with the treatment outcome (see full report) Measurements used for diagnostic purposes or that do not measure UI specifically (see full report)
Quality of life: Generic, sexual function, UI-specific; validated.
Adverse events.
Timing Minimum 4 weeks follow up (since the start of treatment)  
Settings Interventions provided in primary care or specialized clinic or equivalent by any healthcare provider  
Country  Any geographic area  
Study designs For effectiveness outcomes (UI and QoL):   
•randomized controlled trials (RCTs) 
•nonrandomized comparative studies (NRCS) that used strategies to reduce confounding bias, N≥50 women per group (N≥100 total).
For harms outcomes: 
·RCTs
·Any NRCS with N≥50 women per group (N≥100 total) 
·Other, including noncomparative studies (N≥100 women)
Publication language Any Unable to read, translate, or retrieve.
